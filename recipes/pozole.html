Pozole

Description

This recipe will teach you how to cook menudo, a traditional mexican soup that has hominy (treated rehydrated corn) and cow's belly, this dish is usually used as a hangover cure after parties.

Ingredients

  • 3 Pounds of Honeycomb tripe
  • 2 limes
  • 1 head of garlic cut in half
  • 1 medium white onion cut into 4 pieces
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 6 quarts of water
  • 2 15oz cans of ready-cooked white hominy, rinsed and drained
  • 3 small sprigs of mint
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Remove the Honeycomb tripe from the package, drain and rinse.
  2. Place in a large pot or bowl and squeeze the lime juice over the tripe and fill the pot with water.
  3. Let the tripe soak in that lime juice and water solution for 30 minutes, then drain and rinse again.
  4. Drain and rinse again, pat dry with paper towels, and cut into 2-inch pieces.
  5. Place the pieces, with the onion, garlic and bay leaf in a large pot with a lid and fill with the water.
  6. Place on medium-high heat, and bring to a boil. Skim off the foam that will form on the top after a few minutes.
  7. Now, partially cover the pot, reduce the heat to medium-low, and gently simmer for about 2 hours.
  8. Once it's cool enough to handle, chop the meaty parts and discard the bones, return the meat to the pot.
  9. Add the drained and rinsed white hominy along with oregano, and the sprig of mint. Keep cooking slowly, for about another Â½ hour. If the tripe if too chewy, it needs more cooking.
  10. Add salt as needed. Some cooks like to add granulated chicken bouillon to add more flavors to the broth instead of salt.
  11. Serve the White Menudo soup in bowls and garnish.
